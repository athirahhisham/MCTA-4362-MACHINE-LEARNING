Key Predictors

Zenith angle (r=−0.65) and angle of incidence (r=−0.65) are the dominant predictors — they govern how directly sunlight strikes the PV panels.

☀️

Solar Irradiance

Shortwave radiation (r=+0.56) is the primary energy driver — more photons hitting panels = more electricity generated.

☁️

Cloud & Humidity

Total cloud cover (r=−0.33) and relative humidity (r=−0.34) reduce power by blocking and scattering incoming solar radiation.

🤖

Best Model

SVR with RBF kernel wins. The non-linear cosine/exponential physics of PV systems suits the RBF kernel's infinite-dimensional mapping.

📈

Poly Improvement

Polynomial regression (deg=2) improved R² from 0.561 to 0.643 — confirming that quadratic interactions (zenith² × irradiance) capture real physical relationships.

⚡

Practical Impact

SVR's MAE of 337 kW vs MLR's 502 kW is a 33% improvement — commercially significant for energy auction bids and grid balancing decisions.
